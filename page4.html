🌋 Adventure 4: Volcano Power!

Volcanoes are some of the most exciting, fiery, and powerful things on Earth! Let's explore how these smoky mountains work.

What is a Volcano?

A volcano is a mountain that opens downward to a pool of hot, melted rock below the Earth's surface. When this hot liquid rock is still trapped deep underground, it is called magma. When the volcano erupts, the magma pushes its way up and escapes to the outside, where we call it lava!

Did you know? Lava is extremely hot! It can reach temperatures over 2,000 degrees Fahrenheit (1,000 degrees Celsius) — that is ten times hotter than boiling water!

Why Do Volcanoes Erupt?

Inside the Earth, it is very hot. Melted rock and gases mix together. As more gas bubbles form, they get trapped and build up a lot of pressure. Think of it like shaking a big bottle of fizzy soda — if you shake it enough, the pressure builds up and the cap blasts off in a giant spray!

So, steer clear of the bubbling lava, grab your safety goggles, and keep exploring our dynamic planet! 🗺️

🧠 Fun Volcano Quiz!

1. What is the hot liquid rock called when it is still trapped inside the Earth?

🔥 It is called magma! Once it blasts out of the volcano into the open air, its name changes to lava.

2. What builds up inside the volcano that causes it to explode?

💥 Hot gases and pressure build up tightly inside, just like shaking a fizzy soda bottle until it bursts open!
