🌈 Adventure 6: The Rainbow Makers!

Rainbows are like colorful bridges across the sky. But they aren't magic — they are made of science! Let's find out how sun and water work together.

First, We Need Water!

Before a rainbow can form, there must be a lot of water in the sky. This can happen right after it rains, or near a splashing waterfall. The amount of tiny water droplets floating in the air is called moisture. When the sun shines through this moisture, the rainbow adventure begins!

Did you know? A rainbow is actually a full circle! When you stand on the ground, the Earth blocks the bottom half, so we only see an arch. If you fly high in an airplane, you might see a complete circular rainbow!

Bending the Light

Sunlight looks white, but it actually holds all the colors of the rainbow mixed together. As sunlight passes into a floating raindrop, the water bends the light. This bending is called refraction. The bent light splits up into red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet, and bounces back to our eyes!

So, keep your eyes open after the next rain shower, and enjoy the science in the sky! 🌦️

🧠 Fun Rainbow Quiz!

1. What does the air need to have for a rainbow to form?

💧 The air needs moisture, which means lots of tiny water droplets floating in the air from rain or mist!

2. What happens to sunlight when it passes through raindrops to show different colors?

📐 The light bends, which is called refraction. Bending splits the white light into all the colors of the rainbow!
