🧲 Adventure 9: The Magic of Magnetism!

Have you ever played with magnets on your refrigerator? They seem to stick like magic, but they are actually using an invisible force fields! Let's explore magnets.

The Magic Pull

Magnets have an invisible force called magnetism. When you hold a magnet near metal objects like iron nails or steel paperclips, the magnet will attract them. This means the magnet pulls the metal objects right toward it and holds them tight, even without any sticky glue!

Did you know? Our giant planet Earth is actually a giant magnet! It has a magnetic field that spins all compass needles so they point North!

The Pushing Power

Every magnet has two ends: a North Pole and a South Pole. Opposite poles love each other, so North and South attract. But what if you put two North poles together? They will repel each other! This means they push apart. You can try as hard as you want, but you won't be able to force them to touch!

So, stick to your exploration, keep asking questions, and stay curious! 🧭

🧠 Fun Magnet Quiz!

1. What does a magnet do to metal objects like iron or steel?

🧲 The magnet will attract them, pulling the metal objects close and sticking to them!

2. What happens when you try to push two matching magnet poles (like North and North) together?

🚫 They will repel each other, meaning they push away and refuse to touch!
