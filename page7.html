🌧️ Adventure 7: How Raindrops Form!

Have you ever jumped in a rain puddle and wondered where all that water comes from? Let's trace the water cycle journey up to the clouds and back!

Going Up to the Sky!

When the sun shines down on puddles, lakes, and oceans, it heats the water up. As the water gets warm, it turns into an invisible gas called water vapor and rises up into the sky. This process is when water evaporates. It's like the water is taking a secret invisible elevator ride into the sky!

Did you know? An average white, fluffy cloud weighs about 1.1 million pounds (500,000 kilograms)! That's as heavy as 100 giant elephants floating in the air!

Making Clouds and Rain

High up in the sky, it is very cold. The warm water vapor cools down and turns back into tiny liquid water droplets. This process is called condensation. Millions of these tiny droplets stick together to form clouds. When the droplets get too big and heavy to stay in the air, they fall back down to Earth as rain!

So, next time it rains, remember that those raindrops were once part of a lake or ocean somewhere else! ☔

🧠 Fun Rain Quiz!

1. What happens to puddle water when the sun heats it up?

☀️ The water evaporates! It turns into an invisible gas called water vapor and rises high into the sky.

2. How do clouds form in the sky?

☁️ Clouds form through condensation, where water gas cools down and turns back into tiny water droplets that gather together!
